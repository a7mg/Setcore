Formation Evaluation & Production Logging Services

Formation evaluation and production logging are two key techniques used in the oil and gas industry to assess reservoir properties and optimize production from oil and gas wells.

Formation Evaluation

This process involves analyzing geological formations surrounding the wellbore to determine properties such as porosity, permeability, fluid saturation, and lithology. By understanding the characteristics of the reservoir, operators can make informed decisions to maximize hydrocarbon recovery.

Production Logging

Production logging is used to monitor and evaluate the flow of fluids within the wellbore during production. This involves deploying specialized tools downhole to measure parameters such as flow rates, fluid composition, pressure, and temperature at various points along the wellbore. Production logging helps identify issues such as fluid entry from unwanted zones, production profile variations, and the effectiveness of completion techniques such as artificial lift systems. By analyzing production data, operators can optimize well performance, diagnose production problems, and implement remedial actions to enhance production efficiency and maximize reservoir recovery.
